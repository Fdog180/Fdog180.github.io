Question 5 (5 marks)
A multimedia system is the accumulation of several elements. Sound is a major component when it comes to multimedia. How is the sound component of a multimedia system recorded and stored. Also what are the other major components of a multimedia system?

Criteria Mark
Names at least 3 other components 1
Describes sample size 2
Describes sample rate 2

Text, hypertext, numbers, video and animation are all major components of multimedia systems. Sound undergoes a process of digitisation where it is sampled and converted into a binary source. The amplitude of the wave is converted into binary numbers by cutting the wave and measuring it. A sample size refers to the number of bits that are used to store a sample from the analog wave. Sampling rate refers to the amount of time the wave is sampled and converted into binary. Alternatively the sound can be stored as a MIDI file. A MIDI file is where information is stored and transmitted contains data about each sound; time of play, loudness and note identification. A synthesiser is needed to translate a MIDI file into audio for the user.
